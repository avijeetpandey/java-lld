## Double Ledger

What is a Double-Entry Ledger?
In standard accounting, money is never simply "added" or "subtracted." It is always moved. Every transaction must have at least two entries: a Debit (subtracting) from one account and a Credit (adding) to another.

The Golden Rule: For any transaction, the sum of all Debits and Credits must exactly equal zero. If it doesn't, the ledger is corrupted.
